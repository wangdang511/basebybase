Base-By-Base Help
Searching
Back to Main

BBB is able to map sequence searches for strings and regular expressions. Two types of searching are supported - Regular Expression and Fuzzy:

  • Regular Expression (RE) - precise pattern matching

    Regular expressions allow for searching of precise patterns which may include optional sections and/or repeated sequences. For detailed help on regular expressions, please see The Perl Regular Expression page for more information. 

            
    
        	
        		Examples of Regular Expression Searching
        	
        	
    
        		Regular Expression
        		What it matches
        	
    	        	
        	
    
        		ACT
        		ACT
        	
    
        	
    
        		[AC]T
        		A or C followed by T
        	
    
        	
    
        		AC[^T]ACT
        		AC followed by anything BUT a T followed by ACT
        	
    
        	
    
        		ACT*
        		AC followed by 0 or more T's
        	
    
        	
    
        		(ACT)*
        		ACT repeated 0 or more times
        	
    
        	
    
        		(ACT)+
        		ACT repeated 1 or more times
        	
    
        	
    
        		(ACT)?
        		ACT repeated 0 or 1 times
        	
    
        	
    
        		(ACT){n}
        		ACT repeated n times
        	
    
        	
    
        		(ACT){n,}
        		ACT repeated at least n times
        	
    
        	
    
        		(ACT){n,m}
        		ACT repeated at least n times but not more than m times
        	
    
        	
    
        		((AC)[TA]){n}
        		AC followed by T or A - repeated n times
  • Fuzzy - close pattern matching (only for nucleotides)

    With Base-By-Base, it is possible to search within each sequence for a given motif accounting for ambiguities and mismatches. The Fuzzy Motif Search allows users to enter in an expression pattern (see below for an explanation of the pattern grammar used) as well as a maximum number of mismatches tolerated in a search hit. BBB then searches marked sequences for this motif and displays a list of hits sortable by confidence or by location along the sequence. In addition to the ambiguities created by mismatches, users may enter in IUB ambiguity codes, which are also indicated below. 

    	        
    
				
        			Examples of Fuzzy Motif Searching
        		
	        	
    
	        		Fuzzy Expression
	        		What it matches
	        	
    	        	
	        	
    
	        		ACT
	        		an A, C, T pattern
	        	
    
	        	
    
	        		[AC]T
	        		an A or a C followed by a T
	        	
    
	        	
    
	        		{AC}T
	        		anything BUT an A or a C followed by a T
	        	
    
	        	
    
	        		ACT(1,3)
	        		an A then C followed by 1, 2 or 3 T's

    NOTE: When counting mismatches, [] and {} count as a single match or mismatch. As well, if matching T(2,4) and only 1 T is found, this counts as a single mismatch.

    NOTE: This grammar is similar to that of the program fuzznuc which is included in the EMBOSS suite of bioinformatics software.

    Table of IUPAC Ambiguity Codes

    A A T
    C C G
    G G C
    T/U T A
    M A or C K
    R A or G Y
    W A or T W
    S C or G S
    Y C or T R
    K G or T M
    V A or C or G B
    H A or C or T D
    D A or G or T H
    B C or G or T V
